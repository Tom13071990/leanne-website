What Is craniofacial Therapy?

craniofacial therapy focuses on improving the function and coordination of the muscles of the face, jaw, head, and neck. These muscles play a vital role in breathing, chewing, swallowing, speech, and overall facial development.

What Does craniofacial Therapy Treat?

  • Mouth breathing and inefficient breathing patterns
  • tongue posture
  • jaw alignment
  • better sleep
  • clearer speech
  • improved focus, and greater comfort in daily activities

Who Is craniofacial Therapy For?

Myofunctional therapy is appropriate for children, adolescents, and adults. This therapy is especially beneficial for children who experience mouth breathing, feeding challenges, unclear speech, jaw tension, or facial muscle weakness. Through gentle, targeted exercises and guided techniques, craniofacial therapy supports proper muscle function and promotes long-term stability.

What Does the Therapy Process Look Like?

During therapy, gentle hands-on techniques and targeted exercises are used to release tension, improve mobility, and support proper muscle activation. This helps the nervous system recognize healthier movement patterns and positioning.

Kinesiotaping is often used as a supportive tool within craniofacial therapy. When applied to specific facial or cervical areas, the tape provides gentle sensory input that helps guide muscle function, improve awareness, and support symmetry—without restricting movement.

The tape remains in place for several days, allowing the body to receive continuous feedback between sessions. This ongoing input reinforces the work done in therapy and helps maintain progress during daily activities such as breathing, eating, speaking, and resting.

Craniofacial therapy combined with kinesiotaping supports long-term changes by addressing both the structure and function of the muscles, helping children and adults develop healthier, more efficient movement patterns over time.
