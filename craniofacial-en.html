What Is Kinesiotaping Therapy?

Kinesiotaping therapy is a gentle, non-invasive technique that uses elastic therapeutic tape to support muscle function, improve awareness, and encourage healthier movement patterns of the face, jaw, neck, and upper body.

The tape works by providing continuous sensory input to the nervous system, helping muscles function more efficiently without restricting natural movement.

What Does Kinesiotaping Therapy Support?

  • Mouth breathing and inefficient breathing patterns
  • Tongue posture and oral resting position
  • Jaw alignment and facial muscle balance
  • Neck and head posture
  • Swallowing and speech coordination
  • Muscle awareness and symmetry

Who Is Kinesiotaping Therapy For?

Kinesiotaping therapy is suitable for children, adolescents, and adults. It is especially helpful for individuals experiencing muscle weakness, poor oral posture, breathing challenges, jaw tension, or asymmetry in facial or neck muscles.

The technique is gentle, pain-free, and safe for daily wear, making it ideal for supporting therapy goals between sessions.

What Does the Therapy Process Look Like?

During a session, kinesiotape is carefully applied to specific areas of the face, jaw, or neck based on each individual’s functional needs. The application is customized to support muscle activation, relaxation, or alignment.

Once applied, the tape gently lifts the skin and stimulates sensory receptors, helping the nervous system improve muscle coordination and awareness throughout daily activities.

The tape typically remains in place for several days, allowing continuous support between therapy sessions and reinforcing long-term functional changes.

Kinesiotaping is often combined with myofunctional or craniofacial therapy to enhance outcomes and promote lasting improvements.
